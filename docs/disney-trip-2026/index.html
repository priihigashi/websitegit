Disney Kid-First Route Plan (Updated)

No nap / no hotel return version, with visual maps by park cluster and a strict kids-together ride flow (no split-up rides).

Trip order: Thursday = Magic Kingdom, then Hollywood Studios, then Animal Kingdom.
Family setup: grandparents + parents + kids almost 4 and almost 6.
Flow: kid-first clusters, micro-breaks, and everyone stays together for rides.

No-Nap Operating Style

Energy 15–20 minute sit/snack/reset every 90–120 minutes.
Heat Midday = indoor show + lunch + store stroll (instead of hotel).
Flex Repeat rides allowed when kids request it.
Adults Short drink stop rotation while another adult stays with kids.

Magic Kingdom (Thursday)

Best first area: Fantasyland / Storybook Circus.

  1. Start: Dumbo, Pooh, carousel, Under the Sea, Barnstormer (if ready).
  2. Move to Peter Pan + small world.
  3. Castle lunch window.
  4. Adventureland/Liberty loop + optional repeat favorites.

Animal Kingdom

Best first area: Pandora (Avatar) first, then Safari.

  1. Pandora first: Na’vi River Journey (all heights), then decide on Flight of Passage only if both kids meet 44" and are comfortable.
  2. Then move to Safari + Gorilla Falls trail.
  3. Indoor shows: Lion King + Nemo as midday rest.

Lightning Lane + App (Fast Lane)

  1. Install and sign in: My Disney Experience app.
  2. Buy Lightning Lane in-app only: Lightning Lane passes.
  3. Purchase window: eligible Disney hotel guests can buy starting 7 days before stay; other guests starting 3 days before visit, beginning at 7:00 AM ET.
  4. For your plan: use Multi Pass for family-friendly attractions, and decide on Avatar Flight of Passage as a Single Pass only if both kids meet height and comfort.
  5. Theme park reservations: date-based tickets usually do not require park reservations; check your admission type here: Theme Park Reservation Requirements.

Dining reservations generally open 60 days ahead (around 6:00 AM ET); Disney resort guests can book for full stay window (up to 10 nights).
